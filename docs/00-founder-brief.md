# Founder Brief — The Relic

## Problem hypothesis

Podcast listeners consume serialized intellectual content as ephemera. Each episode is heard once, lightly retained for days, then lost. Ideas-driven shows produce cumulative bodies of work, but the medium has **no native primitive for the listener to capture that accumulation.** Note-taking apps presume text input. Audio apps stop at playback. There is a gap between "I listened to 30 episodes of this show" and "I have something to show for it."

## Target user

**Serious listeners of ideas-driven, host-driven podcasts.** Reference shows: Lex Fridman, Dwarkesh Patel, Conversations with Tyler, Acquired, EconTalk, The Knowledge Project, Invest Like the Best.

Behavioral signal:
- Listens to ≥5 hrs/week of long-form interview/essay podcasts
- Has ever screenshotted a moment, taken a note, or shared a clip
- Follows specific shows by name, not the algorithm

Anti-segment:
- Background-music listeners (no signal of compounding intent)
- True-crime / entertainment listeners (different value model)
- Audiobook-only listeners

## Initial market signal (pre-validation)

- Personal observation: most listeners can't recall episodes 3+ weeks later beyond vague themes
- Readwise / Obsidian / Notion are large but text-first; the audio-listener has no peer product
- Substack-style creator products show willingness-to-pay for compounding intellectual output
- "Highlights" features in Spotify / Apple Podcasts are vestigial — under-shipped, signaling un-served demand

**Source quality: tertiary. Confidence: low. This is the founder brief, not the evidence.**

## Constraints

- Solo founder; ~15–20 hr/wk
- Web-only for v1 (no native app, no audio playback)
- Manual concept authoring (no LLM extraction)
- Requires one host partner willing to author a 50-episode arc with disciplined vocabulary
- 6-week timebox to launch decision

## Stakeholder goals

- **Founder:** prove that the artifact compounds listening into something defensible. The relic is the moat.
- **Host partner (downstream):** incremental retention asset for the back catalog; reason for listeners to commit to the show.
- **Listener:** an object that grows; a record of having listened that's worth more than the listening itself.

## Critical assumptions to test in Phase I

1. The pain is real and articulable without prompting.
2. Listeners would engage with a per-show artifact during the listening lifecycle (not just at the end).
3. The artifact is more compelling than a note-taking app (i.e., passive accretion > active capture).
4. There exists a price point or paid-tier relationship listeners would pay for.
5. The "structure emerges" payoff is felt at episode 5–10, not episode 50 (otherwise abandonment risk dominates).

## What I am explicitly NOT validating in Phase I

- Aesthetic preference (Phase III concern)
- Multi-show portability (out of v1 scope)
- Host workflow (decided: hand-authored in v1)
- Distribution channel (Phase IV concern)

## Open path-dependencies

- If interviews reveal listeners want to ANNOTATE (active), not just RECEIVE (passive), the architecture changes materially.
- If listeners only care about the artifact at episode 50+, retention model is broken and the venture is questionable.
- If listeners conflate "relic" with "transcript search," the product is misunderstood — re-narrate.
